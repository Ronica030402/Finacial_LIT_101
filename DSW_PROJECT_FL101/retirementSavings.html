  • Why is it important to save for retirement

    • What does retirement actually mean?
    Retirement is the stage of life at which one decides to leave the workforce behind forever.
    The majority of developed countries, including the United States, have a national pension or benefits
    system in place to augment retirees' wages, and the usual retirement age in these nations is 65.

    Here are four reasons on why you should save for retirement

    1. You Shouldn't Rely on Social Security

      2. Nobody's retirement income should not be based entirely on Social Security.
      The Social Security Administration estimates that after retirement, its payouts replace
      around 40% of the average wage earner's income. According to the majority of financial
      consultants, retirees will require roughly 70% of their pre-retirement earnings to live comfortably.
      You'll still need to come up with around 60% of the money necessary to live well
      after retiring, even with Social Security. Saving for your own retirement gives
      you the chance to design the retirement you want, in addition to not wanting to
      rely on public policy as your retirement plan. Making lifestyle adjustments to save more money now
      could be necessary. But if you do it now, you'll have a better chance of surviving.
